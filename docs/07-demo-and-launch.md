# Pluto demo and launch narrative

## Positioning

Pluto is not a generic AI dashboard and not a project gallery. It helps a school turn a real community need into supervised, evidence-backed work that students can explain and a partner can use.

## Three-minute demo

1. Start on the public landing page. State the promise: one local need becomes a teacher-reviewed mission.
2. As a partner, show the concise challenge intake and the visible **Live AI** or **Template mode** state. Emphasise that a draft is never automatically published.
3. As a teacher, show mission review, safeguards, roster import, and a balanced team proposal. Edit a role and approve the assignment.
4. As a student, show the mission, the current milestone, an approved source, and the Pluto coach. Demonstrate that the coach cites approved evidence and gives a nudge rather than doing the work.
5. As a teacher, release an assessment; as a partner, validate usefulness.
6. Show the Pluto Proof and explain that it is a consent-gated immutable record, not a public student profile.

## Honest launch language

Say **internal pilot** unless managed identity, storage, operational controls, and integrations are live. Say **Template mode** when no model key is configured. Do not describe placeholder integrations, demo accounts, or local storage as production features.

## Suggested launch assets

- Landing hero and mission-preview screenshots.
- A short role-to-role workflow video that includes teacher approval.
- One source-aware coach interaction with its visible mode and citations.
- A consent-safe Pluto Proof example.
- A clear implementation boundary and roadmap for schools.
